My Rationale

I chose the colours of lilac, light blue, black, and white for my website's colour palette. Lilac and light blue are soft and calming colours that can help create a welcoming and approachable atmosphere on a website. Black is a neutral colour that can help create a modern and sophisticated look, and white is a clean and crisp color that can help create a sense of openness and clarity. These colours can also work well together to create a cohesive and harmonious colour scheme for a website. Additionally, these colours are often considered easy to read on screen and can help create a user-friendly and accessible website.

I used grids and wide margins to create a good website layout as they provide a structured and organized framework for the content. Wide margins good website layout by providing a structured and organized framework for the content. Bootstrap grids allowed me to structure different types of elements, such as texts, images, and buttons, in responsive and aesthetic ways.

Different heading sizes and font weight also helped in providing a sense of hierarchy across elements. I used Playfair Display for headings, and Lato for regular text. These serif and san-serif fonts compliment each other well, making the site hierarchy look well-structured.

All photography is my own. On the blog page, I used screenshots of the websites my blog posts were discussing.

Accessibility

To ensure this website met the standards of WCAG 2.0 Accessibility, I added alterntive text to all the images. Buttons have descriptive values on them and form inputs have associated text labels. The reading and navigation order is logical and intuitive. The navigation was accessible as each page was appropriately titled and the purpose of each link could be determined from the link text. Because I made the font-weight thinner, I made sure to increase the font size to 1.1em for all paragraph text, making it readable. The colour contrast ratio (mostly black text on white, or white text on purple) made the colour contrast requirements.
